Provide a detailed decription of pneumothorax:

A pneumothorax is an abnormal collection of air in the pleural space between the lung and the chest wall. It may be caused by pneumonia or fibrosis and other diseases.

Provide a detailed decription of edema:

Pulmonary edema, also known as pulmonary congestion, is excessive liquid accumulation in the tissue and air spaces of the lungs. It will show fluid in the alveolar walls

Provide a detailed decription of atelectasis:

It is the collapse or closure of a lung resulting in reduced or absent gas exchange. Findings can include lung opacification and loss of lung volume.

Provide a detailed decription of pneumonia:

Pneumonia is an inflammatory condition of the lung primarily affecting the small air sacs known as alveoli.  Pneumonia may present with opacities. Complications such as pleural effusion may also be found increasing the diagnostic accuracy of lung consolidation and pleural effusion.

Provide a detailed decription of cardiomegaly:

Cardiomegaly (sometimes megacardia or megalocardia) is a medical condition in which the heart is enlarged.

Provide a detailed decription of calcification:

Pulmonary calcification is a common asymptomatic finding. Pulmonary calcifications are caused mainly by two mechanisms: the dystrophic form and the metastatic form
